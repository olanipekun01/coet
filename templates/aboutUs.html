About the College of Engineering and Technology

The College of Engineering and Technology (COET) at Achievers University, Owo, is a distinguished center for engineering education, established in 2017 with approval from the National Universities Commission (NUC). Our mission is to produce highly skilled engineers with a broad scientific foundation, practical expertise, and a strong entrepreneurial spirit to contribute to national and global development.

The College offers robust Bachelor of Engineering (B.Eng.) programmes in the following disciplines:

  • Biomedical Engineering
  • Civil and Environmental Engineering
  • Computer Engineering
  • Electrical and Electronics Engineering
  • Mechanical Engineering
  • Mechatronics Engineering
Our programmes are designed to provide a comprehensive blend of theoretical knowledge and hands-on practical training, ensuring graduates are prepared for careers in academia, industry, and research. Philosophy

The College of Engineering and Technology is built on the philosophy of empowering engineers to apply scientific principles to real-life challenges, thereby improving the standard of living and sustaining human society. By integrating theory with practice, we aim to produce well-rounded engineers capable of thriving in a rapidly evolving technological world.

Objectives

The primary objectives of the College include:

  • Training engineers with a balanced scientific and practical foundation for professional excellence.
  • Developing graduates who are responsive to the engineering needs of their environment and capable of solving complex problems.
  • Equipping students with the tools to design and produce engineering devices and solutions for societal benefit.
  • Fostering analytical, logical, and innovative thinking to address engineering challenges.
  • Preparing graduates for employment in diverse sectors, including public services, private industries, research institutes, and self-employment.
  • Encouraging further studies in specialized areas of science and technological innovation.
  • Building entrepreneurial capacity among graduates to engage in income-generating ventures.
